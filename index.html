The geography of crime in Malaysia, 2016–2023

Reported crime fell by more than half over eight years — but the decline was anything but even. Twelve connected charts map where crime concentrates, how it shifted, and what remains.

02 · When

A pandemic-shaped decline

Malaysia's crime rate has been on a long decline since 2016. The Movement Control Order in 2020 accelerated that fall sharply. Both assault and property crime dropped over 50% in eight years. But in 2023, the trend reversed for the first time.

03 · What

Each state has its own crime fingerprint

Three in four crimes are property-related — theft or break-ins. But the mix varies widely by state. Kuala Lumpur and Sabah stand out: other theft dominates where most states are led by motorcycle theft. Hover any row to isolate that state's pattern.

04 · Rankings

Rankings and risk

Johor Bahru, Klang and Petaling Jaya drive a disproportionate share of national crime. Every state recorded fewer crimes in 2023 than in 2016 — but the pace of improvement varied widely.

★
The headline: national crime fell 53% from 2016 to 2023, but gains were uneven. Urban west-coast districts still account for over 60% of all cases, motorcycle theft remains a third of incidents, and 2023 saw the first year-on-year increase since records began.
